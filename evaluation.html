Evaluation



UI/UX

We believe we managed to build a robust, easy-to-understand and immersive user experience. Since we established with our client from the beginning that our job was to come up with a working model of a chatbot and not a fully-fledged application ready to be used in the real-world environment, we opted for simplicity and reliability while also keeping a fresh and polished look. The page has as little external noise/information as possible, the main focus being the chat interface which is situated in the middle of the page. It supports all of the capabilities that a basic chat window should have. The bot’s messages and the user’s messages are coloured differently and situated on opposite parts of the window, the chat flows downward and the user can scroll up to see older messages. Upon typing ‘help’, the bot prompts the user with some options in the form of buttons that the user can click on for a more fluid conversation. The bot clearly tells the user what it can and cannot do and in order for the bot to continue with its conversation flow, it requires very little feedback from the user, usually only one word or in the form of ‘yes/no’ type of answer. The overall design is simplistic, reliable and easy to pick up but at the same fresh and professional.

Functionality: We think we managed to build a rather robust piece of software that covers all the major requirements of our client and behaves properly in both expected and unexpected cases. Future work

If the project were to be extended by another three month there would some useful adjustments that could be done in addition to the requirements.

One main area where we see a benefit is adding a speech API, such as Microsoft LUIS. While we decided against it during our development because our bot is targeted at specific knowledge, it might be beneficial for the human computer interaction. The intents (skills of the bot) could be learned by sample data, that can be collected through user testing.

Another aspect is the data storage of the project. It would be ideal to use our findings in NLP and work together with Camden on a database. While we could build a database by ourselves, it would not be of much use for the Council, given they have an existing data pipeline. When working on the data, it could also be possible to make the trained categorizer updateable.
